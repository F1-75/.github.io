About

Engineering with intention.

I'm a mechanical engineering student at Tufts University with a focus on aerodynamics and thermal systems. As aerodynamics project lead for Tufts Electric Racing, I design the full aero and cooling package for our FSAE competition vehicle.

My work spans the entire pipeline — from parametric CAD in OnShape to CFD validation in Ansys Fluent, through composite manufacturing by hand layup. I'm driven by the intersection of fluid dynamics theory and real-world fabrication constraints.

Projects

Selected work.

FSAE Nosecone Geometry Study
2025–26

Comparative CFD analysis of nosecone profiles for the Tufts Electric Racing vehicle. Evaluated drag coefficients, pressure distributions, and stagnation behavior across candidate geometries within FSAE bodywork envelope constraints. Simulated using k-ω SST turbulence model in Ansys Fluent.

Ansys Fluent k-ω SST SpaceClaim OnShape
Radiator Inlet Duct Optimization
2025–26

Designed and optimized the cooling duct geometry feeding the radiator on an electric FSAE vehicle. Balanced pressure recovery, mass flow rate, and packaging constraints imposed by the existing chassis and limited-budget composite manufacturing process.
